THE MILITARY CRYSIS IN THE ESPÍRITO SANTO




The crysis of the public security in 2017 began on February 4, 2017 due to the paralysis of the Military Police of Espírito Santo for claiming the correction of the remuneration of military police officers for the inflation of the period together with the retroactive referring to the absence of this correction since 2010. The manifestations took place throughout the Metropolitan Region of Vitória. After a meeting with the State Government that began on February 24, women and families vacate the barracks on February 25. Since the beginning of the movement 199 people have suffered violent deaths in the state.

British tabloids reported on the strike and violence on 6 February. The Sun and the Daily Mail, among photos of injured people and a bus of the Transcol being burned, highlighted in their reports a citation of social networks, that says "The bad guys are shooting randomly in who passes in the street in the Espírito Santo. My God, what's going on? " In addition, they compare the events to the film The Purge, in which people commit crimes with the temporary suspension of the laws. In the headline, The Sun notices "Lawless Chaos in Brazil with looted stores, buses burned and corpses scattered across the street after police officers strike because of low wages." The Daily Mail, in its online version, shows videos of looting at stores, adding in the headline that "Serve, rape and murder explode on the streets of Brazil after the military police strike in the state of Espírito Santo".

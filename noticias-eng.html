List shows PMs investigated


"Relationship brings names of colonels investigated for supporting strike"

The first list with the name of the military police officers who begin to be punished by the command of the corporation, by quartering was disclosed in the Official Gazette today (February 14, 2017). According to the commander of the PM, Colonel Nylton Rodrigues, they will respond by riot or revolt, which can result in a sentence of up to 20 years.

According to Rodrigues, 703 soldiers will be investigated by a Military Police Inquiry (IPM).

Another 161 will be subject to administrative disciplinary proceedings.

Bus is set on fire at dawn in Aracruz


In the early hours of February 19, 2017 an express bus was set on fire in the Coqueiral neighborhood of Aracruz. The vehicle was parked in front of the driver's house when he was attacked by the arsonists. No one was injured. Officials noticed the attack at 3:55 am, but the bus was already on fire. Police are investigating the case but no suspects have been found. This is the ninth collective torched since the beginning of the military police strike.

Trader killed with 19 shots in bakery


The police investigate whether it is assault or revenge. The owner of the 58-year-old Geraldo Martins Soares bakery was murdered around 5:30 am in Central Carapina in Serra. He had just opened the bakery, when three hooded men invaded and killed him. Witnesses said that the bandits even announced to robbery but soon after they killed the merchant and fled carrying only $ 100.00 and some cell phones. But at the crime scene villagers said he did not accept the curfew given by traffickers in the area, so he could have been killed. But the version is not confirmed by the police. Police investigate the case.

The military police strike ends


After many meetings, there was consensus among women in PMs and government. After many attempts, the government reached an agreement with the women of the military police in a meeting mediated by the State Labor Ministry on February 25. It was 21 days of protest, which began on February 4 and ended on Saturday morning (25), both parties profited from the agreement. "It would have been better if we had not lost so many lives if the government had given in. I think the population has to realize that whoever was And that security could be felt again in our state, "said a representative of the family. "This negotiation has always been happening and we have reached a denominator that has established the end of this movement for the time being.These decisions are good for the institution, the police and society.We were worried about the return of the Military Police to the streets, which is already Happening, "said the colonel. In the consensus it was established that the state government had not opened new Disciplinary Administrative Procedures (PADs) against police officers and had withdrawn from legal proceedings against family members and associations.
